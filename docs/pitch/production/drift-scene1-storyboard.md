# DRIFT — Storyboard

## Scene 1: Pittsburgh Landing → California Study Session

*Sell-version storyboard. Emotional, not technical. The job of this scene is to make someone feel the seamlessness — Drift adds to your day without pulling you out of it — and to land the contrast: social media is cold effort; Drift is warm, alive, already there.*

**Sequence position:** Intro (black screen + statement) → **Scene 1 (this)** → Scene 2 (Gamer) → Scene 3 (Gardener) → Cityscape outro.

**Core contrast to protect across every frame:** Pittsburgh = cold, dark, wet, dated, *manual effort*. California = warm, sunny, in-flow, *effortless*. We never throw a punch at social media directly — we just let it sit there looking like work while Drift feels like a gift.

---

### FRAME 1 — Pittsburgh, the window seat

**Visual:** Tight on a rain-streaked airplane window at dusk. Outside, a gray tarmac, runway lights smearing through the water on the glass. Reflected faintly in the window: a tired traveler (our Pittsburgh friend), head back against the seat. Cabin behind her is dim, half-lit, people starting to stir. Cold blues and grays. It feels late, heavy, a little lonely.

**Audio:** Muffled cabin hum. Rain. No music yet. The world feels muted.

**Emotional beat:** The exhaustion of arrival. The end of a long travel day.

**Pacing:** Hold. Let it feel slow and damp.

**Image prompt:** *Cinematic, rain-streaked airplane window at dusk, gray wet tarmac and blurred runway lights outside, faint reflection of a tired woman resting her head back in a dim airplane cabin, cold blue-gray palette, moody, lonely, photorealistic.*

---

### FRAME 2 — The post (effort)

**Visual:** Her phone lights up as signal returns — a small cold glow on her face. We see her thumbs move. A short, tired message being typed: *"Finally made it to Pittsburgh. Long one. Big conference tomorrow 😮‍💨"*. The act looks like a chore — one more thing before she can rest.

**Audio:** Soft keyboard taps. The "sent" tone. Then quiet again.

**Emotional beat:** Connection here takes *work*. She has to reach out, type, push it into the void.

**Pacing:** Quick but weary.

**Image prompt:** *Close-up of a tired woman's face lit by phone glow in a dim airplane cabin at night, thumbs typing a message, cold blue light, photorealistic, intimate, slightly melancholic.*

---

### FRAME 3 — Into the void (transition)

**Visual:** Her post lifts off the screen as a small card and floats up into a dark, empty space — drifting alone, getting smaller. It's quiet, a little forlorn. As it disappears, the darkness becomes the cut.

**Audio:** A single soft tone as the post goes up. Silence swells.

**Emotional beat:** She did her part. Now it's out there… somewhere. (We don't resolve it yet — that's the hook.)

**Pacing:** This is the bridge. Half a beat of black.

**Image prompt:** *A single glowing social-media post card floating upward into deep black empty space, getting smaller, isolated, minimal, cinematic.*

---

### FRAME 4 — Hard cut: California (the contrast)

**Visual:** Tonal whiplash. Warm afternoon sun floods a room. Roughly 3 PM. A sunlit desk near a window — open textbooks, a highlighter, an iced drink sweating in the light, plants. Our California friend sits cross-legged, relaxed, in flow. The whole frame breathes warmth and ease. This is the exact opposite of Frame 1, and we want the viewer to *feel* the temperature change.

**Audio:** Music fades up — warm, easy, good. This is Drift, already playing. It was always playing.

**Emotional beat:** Alive. Comfortable. Present. Not reaching for anything.

**Pacing:** Let it open up and breathe after the tight, cold Pittsburgh frames.

**Image prompt:** *Warm sunlit study desk by a window, mid-afternoon golden light, open textbooks and highlighters, iced drink, houseplants, a relaxed young woman studying cross-legged, cozy and inviting, photorealistic, warm palette.*

---

### FRAME 5 — She's already listening

**Visual:** We glimpse the Drift interface — minimal, spacious, high-quality. Music playing. In the background, ambient cards shuffle quietly (other small things happening in her world), unobtrusive. She's not looking at it. She's studying. Drift is the room tone of her afternoon, not a thing she's tending.

**Audio:** The track continues, warm and unhurried.

**Emotional beat:** This is the key establishing idea — Drift is *ambient*. She isn't using it. She's living inside it.

**Pacing:** Brief. Plant the interface, then get back to her face.

**Image prompt:** *Minimal, elegant music app interface on a laptop, spacious clean design, a now-playing card and softly shuffling ambient cards, warm sunlit desk in background slightly out of focus, premium and calm.*

---

### FRAME 6 — The break (the DJ arrives)

**Visual:** The music eases down into a break. We don't cut away — we stay with her. Maybe the now-playing card softly transitions to a DJ card. Light, warm, intentional.

**Audio — DJ (warm, neutral, draft VO):** *"…before we roll back into it — looks like someone in our family touched down in Pittsburgh last night. Big conference for her today. Let's send her some good luck."*

**Emotional beat:** The information *finds* her. She didn't search, didn't get pinged, didn't break focus. It arrived inside something she was already enjoying.

**Pacing:** This is the heart of the scene. Let the DJ line land.

**Image prompt:** *Warm minimal music app showing a DJ / now-playing break card, soft glow, sunlit desk behind, premium calm aesthetic.*

> **Note for review:** DJ voice/name TBD per your call (warm; male/female/neutral options). Line above is placeholder copy — written to feel curated and personal, not like a notification.

---

### FRAME 7 — The smile

**Visual:** Her face. She pauses mid-highlight. A small, real, involuntary smile. *She made it.* It's a genuine human beat — not staged delight, just warmth.

**Audio:** DJ line tail, then the music easing back up underneath.

**Emotional beat:** This is the "how did it know that would matter to me right now" moment — the one that makes a viewer want it. The signal was *relevant*, and it came at the right time of her day.

**Pacing:** Hold on the smile a beat longer than feels necessary. This is the sell.

**Image prompt:** *Close-up of a young woman at a sunlit desk pausing while studying, a small genuine warm smile, soft afternoon light, photorealistic, intimate, hopeful.*

---

### FRAME 8 — The card

**Visual:** Because the post made it to voice, it surfaces as a card on her screen — her friend's Pittsburgh message, clean and simple inside the Drift interface. She reaches over and taps it.

**Audio:** Music continues — never stops.

**Emotional beat:** Optional depth. She *can* engage, but on her terms.

**Pacing:** Quick, light.

**Image prompt:** *A clean social post card surfacing inside a minimal music app interface on a laptop, a finger tapping it, warm sunlit desk, premium design.*

---

### FRAME 9 — The overlay (she never leaves)

**Visual:** A window slides up over Drift — a direct portal to the actual post. Crucially, **Drift stays visible/audible behind it.** The music keeps playing. She types a quick reply: *"Good luck tomorrow — you've got this 💛"*. This is the seamlessness made literal: she didn't go to another app, she didn't leave her music, the world she was in never paused.

**Audio:** Same track, uninterrupted, underneath. Soft keyboard taps.

**Emotional beat:** Effortless. The opposite of Pittsburgh's "stop everything and post." Here, responding is a feather-light gesture inside a continuous experience.

**Pacing:** Smooth, unhurried.

**Image prompt:** *An overlay window opening over a minimal music app showing a social post with a short reply being typed, the music interface still visible behind it, warm sunlit desk, seamless premium UI.*

---

### FRAME 10 — Close it out

**Visual:** The overlay collapses away. Drift is right there, still playing, like it never moved. She sets the phone/laptop focus back to her textbook and picks her highlighter back up.

**Audio:** Music swells gently back to full — the break is over, the set returns.

**Emotional beat:** No friction, no residue, no rabbit-hole. She added a moment of connection to her day and lost nothing of her afternoon.

**Pacing:** Light exhale.

**Image prompt:** *A warm study desk, the music app returning to full now-playing view as an overlay closes, a young woman picking her highlighter back up, golden afternoon light, calm and seamless.*

---

### FRAME 11 — Back to flow (closing image)

**Visual:** Wider shot. She's back in her books, sunlight across the desk, Drift quietly alive in the corner. The whole exchange took maybe thirty seconds and never pulled her out of her world. Hold, then we're ready to move toward Scene 2.

**Audio:** The track carries us out, warm.

**Emotional beat:** *This* is what Drift is: it reaches into the life you're already living and makes it a little richer — then gets out of the way.

**Pacing:** Settle, breathe, transition.

**Image prompt:** *Wide cozy shot of a young woman studying at a sunlit desk in the afternoon, relaxed and in flow, a minimal music app glowing softly on her laptop, warm inviting light, photorealistic, peaceful.*

---

**Throughline check for this scene:**
- Contrast lands (cold manual Pittsburgh vs warm effortless California). ✅
- Drift is ambient and live, not a notification. ✅
- The DJ surfaces something *relevant and well-timed* — the "how did it know" beat. ✅
- Responding never breaks the experience — radio never stops, overlay opens over Drift. ✅
- Net feeling: it *added* to her day. No attention theft. ✅
