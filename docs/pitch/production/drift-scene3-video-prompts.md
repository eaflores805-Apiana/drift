# DRIFT — Scene 3 as Video Shot Prompts
### The Gardener · written for a video model (Veo / Gemini), DP-style — one action per clip

**The thesis of this scene (its distinct facet):** where the student *received* and the gamer *shared*, the mother *chooses when to act*. The meaningful thing lands gently into her afternoon, and Drift trusts her to know what to do with it — she doesn't drop everything and run inside; she holds it, stays present, and decides on her own time. This is the **agency-and-respect** beat: a companion hands you meaning and lets it be yours. It's the gentlest of the three on purpose.

**The product, kept honest:** the news (her daughter's promotion) comes from the world she follows, and it arrives in a break *between songs* — the music never stops, nothing interrupts, nothing has to be checked. Her reaction carries the legibility; no cutaway needed (the audio and her face do the work, as with the gamer).

**Rules (same as Scenes 1 & 2):** one action per clip, camera direction first, physical texture not backstory, no readable screens/UI/text. Any phone or earbud is incidental; the music is implied, never shown.

**Template:** `[Shot / camera move] of [subject + physical texture] in [environment] under [light / atmosphere] [film / lens qualities].`

---

## CHARACTER ANCHOR
- **The Gardener:** a woman, 50s, in gardening clothes — worn shirt, sun hat or hair tied back, soil-smudged gloves. Warm, unhurried, at home in her yard. Same person and wardrobe across every shot.

## PALETTE
Warm late-afternoon backyard. Golden, soft, green and alive — dappled light through leaves, dust and pollen drifting. The opposite of the gamer's dark room and a softer cousin of the student's sunlit desk.

---

## THE SHOTS

**Shot 1 — Her afternoon**
> Slow push-in shot of a woman in soil-smudged gardening gloves pressing a seedling into a raised bed, an earbud in one ear, calm and absorbed, in a lush sunlit backyard garden with potted herbs and trailing vines, under warm late-afternoon golden light with pollen drifting in the air, shallow depth of field, anamorphic, 35mm film grain.

*Action: she gardens, content and in flow. (Scene 1's "her afternoon," in the soil.)*

**Shot 2 — The break**
> Static medium shot of the gardening woman pausing mid-motion, head tilting slightly as she listens, hands still resting in the soil, in the warm sunlit garden, under soft golden light with drifting pollen, shallow depth of field, anamorphic, gentle 35mm grain.

*Action: the music has eased into a break between songs; the DJ is speaking. She half-listens, hands still in the dirt. One beat.*

**Shot 3 — The news lands**
> Medium close-up, subtle slow push-in, of the woman going still as something registers — her hands lifting from the soil, her gaze rising and softening, in the warm sunlit garden, under golden light, shallow depth of field, anamorphic, fine grain.

*Action: the meaning arrives. The trowel stops. She hears that her daughter got the promotion. One quiet beat of it landing.*

**Shot 4 — The pride** *(HERO — push hardest here)*
> Static close-up of the woman straightening up, dirt on her gloves, a slow proud smile spreading as her eyes warm and lift toward the light, in the sunlit garden, under golden backlight catching loose strands of hair, shallow depth of field, creamy bokeh, anamorphic, 35mm film look.

*Action: pride, not just happiness — the specific warmth of a parent. "She made it." Hold here; this is the sell.*

**Shot 5 — She holds it** *(the agency beat — what makes this scene distinct)*
> Medium shot of the woman standing in her garden, gloves resting at her sides, taking a slow breath as a small resolved nod settles over her, in the warm afternoon light, under golden glow with drifting pollen, shallow depth of field, anamorphic, 35mm grain.

*Action: she doesn't rush inside. She holds the moment, decides — she'll call her daughter when she's done here — and stays present. This is the respect-for-her-pace beat the other two scenes don't have.*

**Shot 6 — Back to the soil**
> Slow pull-back shot of the woman kneeling again to her garden bed and returning to her work, a faint proud smile lingering, in the lush sunlit backyard, under warm late-afternoon light, shallow depth of field, anamorphic, 35mm film grain.

*Action: she returns to the afternoon, which never stopped. Camera pulls BACK (Shot 1 pushed in), so this reads as a distinct closing beat. The day was hers, and it just got better.*

## NOTES FOR THE EDIT
- The DJ voiceover carries the actual news (the song note, a local cue, then gently the daughter's promotion). The plates carry the *feeling* — pride, and the quiet dignity of being trusted to act on your own time.
- Order: 1 → 2 → 3 → 4 → 5 → 6.
- Shot 5 is the one most attempts would skip, and it's the whole point of this scene. Don't cut it — without it, the gardener is just a happier version of the student. With it, she's the proof that Drift respects your pace.
- Keep it the gentlest of the three. No energy spike, no sharp cut — this scene breathes.
