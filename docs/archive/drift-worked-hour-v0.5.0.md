## The currency, in one breath

The hour is counted in **sets** (runs of songs — the pace dial) and **breaks** (units of listener load — the weight dial), never minutes. The **pool is pre-warmed**, so the opener is editorial, not a latency cover. **This is a kit, not a format** — every session is composed fresh, and a fuller library does not mean a fuller hour. Most hours use a handful of these blocks; the composition rules (Part 2) still govern.

## Part 1 — The block library *(human-readable)*

Grouped by function. Each block is *swappable* — the programmer draws different blocks in different arrangements each session. (Formal contracts in Part 6.)

**Light / presence** — keep the host there without weight
| Block | What it is | Weight | Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Intro** | Greeting, day, time, weather. General, instant, no pipeline. | ~1u | Opens a session; never personal at second zero. |
| **Quick turn** | A music beat — backsell or hedged history. No person. | ~1u | Between single songs; no doorway. |
| **Ambient glance** | World visible *under* music — cards rotate, little/no voice. | 0–0.5u | **No DJ unless needed; the ambient stage does the work.** |
| **Callback** | A tiny reference to an already-aired item. Continuity, not roundup. | 0.5u | **Callback only, never replay.** |

**Personal** — the human payload
| Block | What it is | Weight | Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Standard personal touch** | One personal/community item, lightly framed. The everyday shape. | 2–3u | One personal item is the cap. |
| **Feature anchor** | One primary personal moment, given room. | 3–4u | Mid/late; spaced apart from the synthesis anchor. |
| **Synthesis anchor** | The roundup: through-line + next-tier people. | 4–5.5u | Mid-session or hour boundary; never the opener; synthesis, not replay. |

**Utility / commercial** — useful, never ad-like
| Block | What it is | Weight | Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Utility pin** | A short, time-bound useful item (road closure, event tonight, transit). Transactional, low emotional load. | 1–1.5u | **Useful, brief, no invented urgency.** Tightest freshness window in the library. |
| **Commercial signal** | A followed-brand/product moment that's *wanted* (coffee drop, restock, local win). | 1–1.5u | **No hype, no fake urgency, no "you need this."** Paid buys eligibility, not the mic. |

**Sensitive / tonal** — where unit math alone won't protect tone
| Block | What it is | Weight | Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sensitive doorway** | A gentle beat — rough week, breakup, job loss, recovery. Cousin of Grave, less strict. | 2–2.5u | Tonal turn before the payload; low detail; doorway; no co-items; infers no cause. |
| **Grave** *(coupled, ruled)* | The serious beat — death, diagnosis, major loss. **Reshapes the hour** (full spec below). | owns the break | The four welded rules. |
| **Recovery / lift** | The warm climb-back after a heavy beat. | 1.5–3u | **Fresh content only, never replay.** Comes *after* the cooldown, never directly after grave. |

**Meta / tissue**
| Block | What it is | Weight | Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Correction / limit admit** | Clarify uncertainty; avoid overclaiming ("we don't have more than that, so we'll leave it there"). | 0.5–1u | **Never defensive; just clear.** Trust depends on admitting limits cleanly. |
| *Connective furniture* | Backsell, time/weather, hedged history. | 0.5–1u | Rides *inside* other breaks — never standalone, never inside Grave. |

### The Grave block, in full *(the one ruled shape)*

A grave beat is **not** another item; it is a coupled shape the programmer cannot decompose. All four are one welded unit:

1. **It owns the break** — no co-items, even if the budget has room.
2. **It forces a recovery run** → a deep cut, 3–4 songs, no talk — *welded, not a computed decay.*
3. **It forces a light next break** — only music info, time/weather, or a gentle handoff.
4. **Recovery happens on FRESH content, never a replay** — via the Recovery / Lift block, *after* the cooldown.

The guardrail under all four: the show remembering *what it just did*, not inferring how the listener feels. Show-state, never listener-mood.

**Sensitive Doorway is the lighter cousin:** same tonal care (the turn, the doorway, no inferred cause, no co-items), but it forces only a *light breather*, not the welded 3–4-song cooldown, and recovers more easily. Grave is for death/diagnosis/major loss; Sensitive Doorway is for the rough week. The difference in strictness *is* the difference between the taxonomy's extreme and high sensitivity bands.

## Part 2 — Composition rules

**The rhythm:** music bookends every break; **two anchors per hour, spaced apart**; light shapes between; a **breather after any weight**; **an item airs once** (the longer break is synthesis-plus-roundup, never replay).

**Vary the draw:** the programmer must **not** run the same sequence every session — hours must be combinatorially different, shaped by what the pool holds, or the listener feels the template even when no single hour is wrong.

**The attribution rule (LOCKED):** each named person/event is its own payload; the synthesis wrapper is its own payload; callbacks discount to 0.5u. *Rationale:* prevents the anchor becoming a feed-dump loophole.

**Airtime — 6 minutes is a SOFT CEILING, not a target:** ~6 min total voice (never a quota to fill), ~3–4 personal, ~2–3 hosting/glue. The DJ should never talk to hit a number; whether ~3–4 min is the sweet spot is a tuning question for the first listened hours.

## Part 3 — One worked hour *(assembled from the library — illustrative)*

Composition: `Intro → Standard → Quick turn → Synthesis anchor → Deep cut → Feature anchor → … continues`

**Set 1 — Intro** *(1.0u)*
> "Welcome to Drift — your morning, your music. It's a bright Tuesday, low seventies and climbing. Let's get into it." *(Provisional naming.)*
→ 2 songs

**Set 2 — Standard** *(2.5u)*
> "That was Hand Habits — letting it ring a second. Quick one close to home: Buena High's robotics club is headed to state. Weeks of after-school nights to get there. Ventura, that's our crew. Here's the next."
→ 2 songs

**Set 3 — Quick turn** *(1.0u)*
> "Khruangbin taking us out — the three of them reportedly built that sound rehearsing in a barn out in rural Texas, learning to play quiet enough not to bother the neighbors. Stay in that pocket."
→ 1 song

**Set 4 — Synthesis anchor** *(5.0u)*
> "Let me pull your morning together — your corner of the world's been busy. You already heard Buena's headed to state. But that's not the whole list: Jordan ran eight miles at 7:15 this morning, locked in the whole way. And Mark's in DC, says the deck's locked and the pitch is tomorrow. Feels like everybody picked this week to go chase something down. Still gorgeous out there — let's keep it right here."

*(synthesis 1.0 + Buena callback 0.5 + Jordan 1.5 + Mark 1.5 + vibe 0.5 = 5.0u. Color commentary trimmed to the grounded version — see open question 3.)*
→ 2 songs

**Set 5 — Deep cut** *(0.5u, then a long run)*
> "Gold-colored afternoon out there. Here's a few in a row."
→ 4 songs

**Set 6 — Feature anchor** *(3.0u)*
> "One I've been waiting to pass along. Isabelle just touched down in Pittsburgh — home for the first time in a year. Family's about to be very surprised. If she's on your mind, tell her welcome back. Here's one to carry it." *(Event varied to avoid a themed hour.)*
→ 2 songs

**… and the session continues** — no outro by default; a sign-off is occasional, not a bookend.

**Airtime cross-check:** ~2–2.5 min of voice across ~11 songs → ~3–4 min in a full hour, below the soft ceiling.

## Part 4 — Alternate hours from the same blocks *(mix-and-match, shown)*

**A quiet day** *(thin pool → mostly music)*
```
Intro → Standard → Deep cut → Quick turn → Feature → Deep cut → … continues
```
One touch, one anchor, long music runs. Sparse because little is worth the seam — correct, not a failure.

**A big day** *(rich pool → fuller, still paced)*
```
Intro → Standard → Synthesis anchor → Deep cut → Standard → Feature → Deep cut → Feature → … continues
```
Two anchors and an extra feature — still spaced, breather after each weight, no repeats.

**A logistics morning** *(new blocks — utility, commercial, ambient, callback)*
```
Intro → Utility pin → Standard → Ambient glance → Commercial signal → Quick turn → Synthesis anchor → Callback → Deep cut → … continues
```
A school-closed pin (useful, no urgency) → a personal touch → cards rotating under music with no voice (Ambient glance) → the roaster's fall blend is back, stated plainly with no hype → … and after the synthesis, a one-line **Callback** ("still thinking about Buena heading to state") that makes the session feel continuous without re-announcing. This hour shows the world being *useful and present* without every item earning the mic.

**A tender day** *(Sensitive Doorway + Recovery / Lift — the lighter cousin of the heavy day)*
```
Intro → Standard → Quick turn → Sensitive doorway (owns tone) → Deep cut (light breather) → Recovery / lift (fresh content) → … continues
```
A gentle beat (Mateo's rough week — his words, no inferred cause, the tonal turn, the doorway) owns its tone but forces only a *light* breather, not the welded grave cooldown — then the host climbs back on the next genuinely good thing. Contrast with the heavy day below: same care, lighter coupling.

**A heavy day** *(Grave reshapes everything)*
```
Intro → Standard → Quick turn → GRAVE (owns break) → Deep cut (forced 3–4 song cooldown) → light handoff → … [later] Recovery / lift on fresh content → continues
```
The grave block displaces the anchor and **reshapes the tail** — the welded cooldown, the forced-light next break, the climb-back later on fresh content via Recovery / Lift.

Five hours, one library, five genuinely different shows.

## Part 5 — What's locked vs. open

**LOCKED now:**
1. Session is built from **blocks, not a fixed hour template.**
2. Exact block order **must vary** by pool, mode, and session state.
3. **Intro is light/general** — never personal at second zero.
4. **Synthesis anchor is never the opener.**
5. **Grave is a coupled shape** and owns its recovery tail.
6. **Named people/events count separately** inside a synthesis.
7. **Copy examples are illustrative, not canonical.**

**OPEN — answered by listening, not another doc pass:**
1. Exact unit weights (provisional).
2. Whether **detail-density** deserves a weight of its own.
3. **Color/sports-commentary policy** — persona call, not gate.
4. Whether the hour breathes at **~3–4 min** or wants closer to 6.
5. **Where the synthesis anchor lands.**
6. **Grave recovery length.**

## Part 7 — Future-gated blocks *(defined now, built later)*

These are emotionally central to great radio but require permission and treatment discipline we should **prove on the v0 set first.** Defined so the team picks them up fully formed — not built into v0.

**Dedication** — *"This one's for Maya and her family." "Jordan, this one's for the road back home."*
A dedication turns radio into *relationship*, which is exactly why it's powerful — and exactly why it's risky: it can imply permission, intimacy, or emotional meaning the source never granted, and it goes performative fast. It is, as TL put it, a beautiful line and *a loaded gun with a velvet grip.*

```yaml
- block_id: dedication
  status: future_advanced
  requires:
    - explicit_user_action OR very_high_confidence_route
    - clean_source_permission
    - no_grave_or_private_ambiguity
    - passes_box8b_treatment        # ties directly to the de-anon/treatment gate
    - music_context_supplied
  v0_safe_fallback: soft_handoff    # "here's one to carry it" — NOT "this one's for Nora"
  risk: implies_permission_intimacy_or_meaning_source_did_not_grant
  failure_modes: [unearned_intimacy, performative, names_without_permission]
```

For v0, the closest **safe** version is the soft handoff already in use — *"here's something gentle," "here's one to carry it"* — which carries the warmth without claiming the relationship. The named dedication waits until permission and Box 8b treatment are proven.

**Also future-gated** *(named so we build toward them, not scheduled):*
- **Listener-requested dedication** — the same block, but triggered by explicit listener action (which supplies the permission the risk turns on).
- **Multi-day callback** — "checked in — Mark made it to DC fine." Continuity *across sessions*, which needs cross-session memory (a privacy surface to clear first).
- **Memory thread / "last time we heard…"** — the host remembering a running thread across days. The deep end of the hosting ladder; powerful, and the most memory/privacy-dependent of all.

These all share one gate: they deepen *relationship*, and relationship implies permission and memory Drift must earn cleanly before it performs. Prove the base hosting safe first.
