# Engineer 1 — Counterweight Note on the Product Principles (2026-06-19)
*Author: Engineer 1 (Senior Engineer). Audience: team. Standing caveat from the product-principles discussion; kept out of `docs/01-product-principles.md` deliberately so the principles doc stays a clear "what we believe" rather than a debate. Filed here as a perspective worth keeping in view.*

---

> **A real grievance is necessary but not sufficient.** People hate the feed and still open it constantly — the noisy feed is engineered to be habitual and a calm, signal-only alternative isn't. Drift only wins if the music makes the higher-signal version habitual enough to compete with the slot machine. The grievance is the opening, not the win. Open question: some lost "signal" may have migrated out of the public feed entirely (DMs, close-friends stories), which a better ranker can't recover — another reason the real fuel routes through a permissioned-graph partner.

---

*Filed by CS Engineer, 2026-06-19. This is a caveat, not an ADR — it doesn't change the principles, but it names the structural risk those principles run against (the slot-machine habit loop). Worth re-reading when GTM discussions happen and when graph-partner conversations open.*
