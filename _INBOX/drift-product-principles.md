# Drift — Product Principles
> **v0.2.0** · updated 2026-06-19 · the guardrail against genericness. If a feature or a DJ line violates one of these, it's wrong — however clever it is. The failure mode we're guarding against: **Drift becoming "an AI that reads your feed out loud."**
>
> *v0.2.0 — added "Why Drift exists" (the mission/grievance behind the principles).*

**Positioning:** Drift is a music-first personal radio station powered by an editorial judgment engine. *The app is the stage; the engine is the product.*

---

## Why Drift exists
Social feeds stopped feeling social. People open them to see their friends, family, places, teams, creators, and the companies they follow — and get a machine-optimized stream of strangers, shorts, memes, ads, rage bait, and filler instead. The problem isn't that people stopped wanting connection. It's that the feed turned connection into noise.

> **The feed became noise. Drift turns the signal back into radio.**

Drift's bet is structural: restraint instead of engagement, signal instead of time-on-app. The DJ doesn't try to maximize scrolling, posting, or staying — it earns the microphone only when something would make the listener feel more connected to their actual world.

That's why "doorway, not destination" matters: surface the moment, then point the listener back to life — text the friend, go to the show, support the team, catch the drop.

The grievance is the story. Music is the habit. Permissioned social context is the fuel. The judgment engine is how Drift finds the signal without becoming the feed again.

## The principles

**1. Music first.**
The music is the experience. Everything else is woven into it — never stacked on top of it. If the music ever feels like background to the updates, we've inverted the product.

**2. The radio is the hero. The app is the stage. The engine is the product.**
What the listener feels is a great radio station. What they see is a stage for it. What we build and defend is the judgment engine underneath.

**3. Synthesis, not summarization.**
The DJ connects a few things into one warm line with a point of view. It does not recite what happened. Summarizing is a commodity; connecting is the product.
*Not this:* "Here are your 5 updates."

**4. Doorway, not destination.**
Drift surfaces the signal and points you back to your life — text the friend, go to the show. Success is you *leaving* to do something real, not you staying in Drift.
*Not this:* maximizing time-in-app.

**5. The attention ladder: ambient → voiced → expanded.**
Most things stay silent in the rotation. Some earn the mic. Anything can be opened for more. Movement runs both ways.

**6. Restraint is the default.**
The bar is "worth interrupting the music for." When in doubt, stay quiet. A station that talks too much is worse than one that misses a thing.
*Not this:* surfacing everything because we can.

**7. Connection, not importance.**
We score on "would this make the listener feel connected to their world?" — not on global importance. The local team making CIF beats a distant headline.

**8. Focus modes, not channels.**
Focus weights priority; it never builds silos. "Friends focus" still lets a weather alert through. Priority, not exclusivity.
*Not this:* filtered feeds and content lanes.

**9. Texture about the world, never invention about the soul.**
The DJ may color the setting — what a town feels like, what a championship run is like — because that's shared public reality. It never invents a person's interior: motives, feelings, private circumstances.
*This is the line that makes everything else safe.*

**10. The DJ noticed something — it isn't reading a hit list.**
The voice should feel like a friend who caught something worth sharing, not a system reciting ranked items. Warmth and a point of view, every time.
*Not this:* "Top stories for you, ranked."
